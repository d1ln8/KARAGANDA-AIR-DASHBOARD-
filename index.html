Breathing in Karaganda

A data-driven investigation into air pollution in Kazakhstan’s industrial city

The Invisible Problem

Karaganda is one of Kazakhstan’s major industrial cities. But behind economic development lies a long-term environmental issue: air pollution.

Rising Air Pollution (PM2.5 over time)

Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) has consistently exceeded WHO safe limits.

Where pollution comes from

Industrial zones and coal heating areas are the main sources of emissions.

Why is air pollution so high?

  • Coal-based heating systems in households
  • Industrial mining and metallurgy
  • Old infrastructure and emissions control limits
  • Winter temperature inversion trapping pollution

“Air pollution in Karaganda is not a short-term problem. It is a structural issue connected to energy dependency on coal.”

What residents feel

During winter, air becomes visibly heavier. Many residents report difficulty breathing, irritation in eyes, and reduced outdoor activity.

Conclusion

Karaganda’s air pollution reflects a deeper systemic challenge: balancing industrial growth with environmental health.
